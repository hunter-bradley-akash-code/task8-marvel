# **MQ135 Gas Sensor**

### INTRODUCTION
The MQ135 Gas Sensor is widely used for air quality monitoring and environmental sensing applications. It is commonly integrated with microcontrollers like 
Arduino and ESP-based IoT boards to measure the presence of gases such as ammonia, nitrogen oxides, smoke, alcohol vapors, and carbon dioxide (indirectly).
The MQ135 is a Metal Oxide Semiconductor (MOS) type gas sensor designed for detecting air quality levels. It belongs to the popular MQ series of gas sensors.

### Detectable Gases
* Ammonia (NH₃)
* Nitrogen Oxides (NOx)
* Alcohol
* Benzene
* Smoke
* Carbon Dioxide (CO₂ – indirectly measured)
- - -
### Technical Specifications
* Operating Voltage: 5V DC
* Output Type: Analog and Digital
* Preheat Time: 24–48 hours (recommended for accuracy)
* Detection Range: 10 ppm – 1000 ppm (depending on gas type)
- - -
### CONSTRUCTION
The MQ135 gas sensor is a metal oxide semiconductor (MOS) type chemiresistive sensor designed to detect air quality-related gases. 
Its internal construction consists of a ceramic (alumina) tube that acts as a strong heat-resistant insulating base. Wrapped around this ceramic tube is a thin layer of tin dioxide (SnO₂)
which serves as the primary sensing material. Tin dioxide is a semiconductor whose electrical resistance changes when exposed to certain gases. 
Inside the ceramic tube a nickel-chromium (Ni-Cr) heating coil is embedded. This heater is essential because the sensing reactions occur effectively only at elevated temperatures, typically between 200°C and 300°C. 
The heater ensures that the sensing layer maintains a stable operating temperature for accurate gas detection. The entire sensing element is enclosed within a stainless steel mesh cap that protects the sensor from dust and mechanical damage while still allowing gases to diffuse inside. 
Electrical electrodes are connected to the sensing layer to measure changes in resistance and provide output signals to external circuits.
- - -
### WORKING
The MQ135 operates on the principle of resistance variation in a metal oxide semiconductor (SnO₂) sensing layer. When powered, the internal heater raises the sensor temperature to its optimal operating range. 
In clean air, oxygen molecules are adsorbed onto the surface of the tin dioxide layer where they capture free electrons from the semiconductor. 
This process increases the sensor’s resistance by forming a depletion region.
When pollutant gases such as ammonia, alcohol vapors or nitrogen oxides are present they react with the adsorbed oxygen ions and release the trapped electrons back into the sensing material. This reduces the depletion region, decreases the resistance, and increases conductivity. 
The resulting resistance change produces a variation in output voltage which can be measured using a microcontroller’s analog input. 
By comparing the resistance in polluted air with the baseline resistance in clean air the relative gas concentration can be estimated.

### PIN CONFIGURATION
The MQ135 module typically has 4 pins:

* VCC – 5V power supply
* GND – Ground
* AOUT – Analog Output
* DOUT – Digital Output
- - -
### Sample Arduino Code
``` c++
int gasSensor = A0;
int sensorValue = 0;

void setup() {
  Serial.begin(9600);
}

void loop() {
  sensorValue = analogRead(gasSensor);
  Serial.print("Gas Sensor Value: ");
  Serial.println(sensorValue);
  delay(1000);
}
```

### Advantages and Limitations
> Advantages
* Low cost
* Easy to interface
* Dual output (Analog + Digital)
* Wide gas detection capability
* Suitable for IoT integration
> Limitations
* Requires calibration
* Not highly selective to one specific gas
* Influenced by temperature and humidity
* High power consumption due to heater
- - - 
### CONCLUSION
The MQ135 gas sensor is an affordable and versatile device for air quality monitoring applications. 
Its simple working principle and compatibility with microcontrollers make it ideal for beginners as well as intermediate-level embedded system projects.
Although it requires proper calibration for accurate measurements it remains one of the most popular choices for environmental sensing and IoT-based monitoring systems.
As environmental awareness increases globally, sensors like MQ135 play an essential role in building smarter, safer, and pollution-aware systems.
